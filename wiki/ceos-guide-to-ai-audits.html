The CEO's Guide to AI Audits

How to map exactly where your hours are bleeding and identify high-ROI automation opportunities.

Most founders doing $10M–$20M a year know they have an efficiency problem. They feel it in the 14-hour workdays, the declining margins, and the constant "check-ins" from staff.

But when they try to fix it, they start with tools. They buy a subscription to an AI writing assistant or a new CRM. This is a mistake.

AI doesn't solve problems; systems do. And before you can build a system, you must conduct an Audit.

Step 1: The Time Audit (The "Bleed" Map)

The first step isn't looking at your tech; it's looking at your payroll. Specifically, where is the most expensive time in your business being spent?

  • The Reporting Trap: How many hours a week does your management team spend pulling data from one system and putting it into another?
  • The Approval Bottleneck: How many times a day does a process stop because it's waiting for a "yes" from the owner?
  • The Data Entry Tax: What percentage of your staff's day is spent on non-billable administrative work?

Step 2: The Decision Audit

Every founder has a "decision load"—the number of times a day they must make a choice for the business to move forward. If your load is over 40 items a week, you are a bottleneck.

During an AI Audit, we identify rule-based decisions. If a decision follows a logic (e.g., "If the margin is above 15% and the vendor is X, then approve"), it can be handled by Layer 02 — Automation.

The 70/30 Rule

In a typical $12M service or logistics company, 30% of all staff time is spent on tasks that are 100% automatable today. This is the "AI Gap." Closing this gap is usually the difference between a 10% margin and a 25% margin.

Step 3: The Tribal Knowledge Audit

Where does the "how-to" live in your business? If it's in the heads of 3 or 4 key employees, your business is at risk.

We audit your SOPs and documentation. If your documentation hasn't been updated in 6 months, it doesn't exist. An AI Operating System captures this knowledge into Layer 03 — Knowledge, making it searchable and actionable for everyone on the team instantly.

Why an Audit is the First Step

Skipping the audit is why 90% of AI implementations fail. Without a map of your workflows, you are just throwing tech at a wall and hoping it sticks.
